# VOCAB (/)

## Core terms

### CANONIC
The constitutional programming paradigm defined by the root scope.

### CANON
The constraints artifact that defines what must be true for a scope; co-equal with VOCAB.

### VOCAB
The meanings artifact for a scope; co-equal with CANON. It spans CANON, SPEC, and README, defining their terms and defining itself.

### README
The narrative artifact that explains a scope to humans.

### SPEC
A declarative artifact that defines patterns or constraints not captured by CANON.

### template
A reusable blueprint for instantiating a governance artifact.

### machine
The validation engine layer that performs mechanical checks and correction downstream of CANONIC.

### triad
The required set of governance artifacts in every scope: CANON.md, VOCAB.md, README.md.

### scope
The area governed by a CANON and its triad.

### axiom
A single, immutable CANON statement that must hold for a scope.

### axioms
Plural of axiom.

### co-equal
The status of two canonical artifacts having equal authority in a scope.

### inheritance
The declared linkage from a scope to the scope it inherits from.

### final
The property that inherited axioms cannot be overridden by downstream scopes.

### coherence
The property that CANON statements do not contradict.

### compliance
The property that structural requirements (triad + inheritance declaration) are satisfied.

### governance
The state where coherence and compliance both hold.

### governed
A scope that is both coherent and compliant.

### invalidity
The state of not being governed.

### add
To include a term or artifact in a scope.

### added
Past tense of add.

### all
Without exception.

### apply
To use a SPEC to constrain an inherited scope.

### applies
Present tense of apply.

### cannot
A statement of prohibition.

### confirm
To validate that a statement holds.

### constraint
A rule stated by SPEC or CANON that limits valid scopes.

### constraints
Plural of constraint.

### consumption
Downstream use of a SPEC or CANON.

### contain
To hold artifacts within a scope.

### contains
Present tense of contain.

### contradict
To conflict with an inherited CANON statement.

### contradicting
Present tense of contradict.

### cover
To include required terms from another artifact.

### declare
To state inheritance or scope linkage in CANON.

### declared
Past tense of declare.

### declares
Present tense of declare.

### define
To add a term to VOCAB or a rule to CANON or SPEC.

### defines
Present tense of define.

### definition
A VOCAB entry that gives meaning to a term.

### definitions
Plural of definition.

### describe
To explain a scope in README.

### describes
Present tense of describe.

### downstream
A scope that declares inheritance from another scope.

### ensure
To require validation of a statement or constraint.

### every
Without exception.

### exist
To be present in a scope.

### exists
Present tense of exist.

### finality
The property that inherited axioms cannot be overridden.

### first
The ordering that places SPEC before CANON and VOCAB in derivation.

### host
To keep and provide artifacts such as templates.

### hosts
Present tense of host.

### how
The manner in which something is related or done.

### include
To require that a term or artifact is present.

### inform
To guide an inherited scope by providing a SPEC.

### informs
Present tense of inform.

### inherit
To take axioms from a parent scope.

### inherited
Taken from a parent scope.

### inherits
Present tense of inherit.

### intent
The declared purpose of a scope.

### itself
The same artifact referring to itself.

### layer
A scope in the inheritance chain with a distinct role.

### layers
Plural of layer.

### md
The file extension for Markdown.

### note
A short statement recorded for downstream use.

### notes
Plural of note.

### override
To replace a final axiom in a downstream scope.

### overridden
Past tense of override.

### paradigm
The overarching model defined by CANONIC.

### part
A component within a scope or artifact.

### point
To reference another artifact.

### points
Present tense of point.

### provide
To supply templates or constraints for downstream use.

### provides
Present tense of provide.

### purpose
The intent for a scope.

### reference
To point from one artifact to another for definitions.

### references
Present tense of reference.

### relate
The statement of how artifacts connect within a scope.

### root
The topmost scope in an inheritance chain.

### scopes
Plural of scope.

### span
To cover or include terms from another artifact.

### spans
Present tense of span.

### specify
To state relations or constraints in SPEC.

### states
Present tense of state.

### structure
The declared way CANON, VOCAB, SPEC, and README fit together in a scope.

### templates
Plural of template.

### term
A deliberate word or phrase used in CANON or SPEC that must be defined in VOCAB.

### terms
Plural of term.

### terminate
To end an inheritance chain.

### terminates
Present tense of terminate.

### trace
A list that shows how axioms are derived.

### use
To employ an artifact or term.

### used
Past tense of use.

### validate
To perform validation against CANON or SPEC.

### validation
The process of checking a scope against CANON or SPEC.

### verify
To validate a statement or constraint.

### when
At the time or condition specified.

### which
The specific item being referenced.

### introspection
The requirement that VOCAB defines itself and the CANON, SPEC, and README it supports.

End of VOCAB.
