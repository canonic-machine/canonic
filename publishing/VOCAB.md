inherits: /canonic/

## Content Concepts

### dissemination

The complete distribution of a publication across all required platforms in the stack.

---

### preprint

A manuscript deposited on a preprint server (arXiv, medRxiv, SSRN) before peer review; establishes timestamp authority.

---

### cross-registration

The practice of linking publications across multiple platforms to create an evidence mesh.

---

### evidence mesh

The interconnected web of platform references that validates a publication's authenticity and reach.

---

### platform routing

The process of directing papers to domain-appropriate preprint servers based on sublanguage.

---

### dissemination stack

The complete set of platforms required for valid publication: Preprint → LinkedIn → Twitter → Company.

---

### publication sequence

Required order for dissemination across platforms.

---

### timestamp authority

The preprint server's role as the authoritative source for publication date; establishes priority.

---

### arXiv

Preprint server for physics, mathematics, computer science, and AI papers.

---

### medRxiv

Preprint server for health sciences and clinical research papers.

---

### SSRN

Social Science Research Network; preprint server for law, economics, and policy papers.

---

### LinkedIn

Professional social network for credibility and network dissemination.

---

### Twitter

Social media platform (X) for viral reach and public engagement.

---

### DOI

Digital Object Identifier; persistent identifier for publications.

---

### v1

First version papers covering CANONIC core: the platform and APPSTORE.

### v1 papers (canonic core)

Catalogue of papers covering CANONIC core releases.

---

### v2

Second version papers covering sublanguages: CANONIC.MEDICINE, CANONIC.LAW, etc.

### v2 papers (sublanguages)

Catalogue of papers covering CANONIC sublanguage releases.
